## Case Study: Ransomware

**What the crime is:**
Ransomware is a type of computer virus designed to deny access to a computer system or data until a ransom is paid. The latest iteration of ransomware involves stealing important data before encryption and threatening to leak it if the ransom demand is not met.

**How it typically happens:**

1. **Infiltration:** An unsuspecting employee clicks on a spear phishing email that resembles a company memo and unwittingly downloads the ransomware onto the computer system.
2. **Reconnaissance:** The attacker navigates through the network to identify important data. The key to this attack is identifying and disabling the backups of the data.
3. **Exfiltration:** The attacker steals important company data and uploads it to their server to be used as leverage against the company.
4. **Encryption:** The ransomware begins to encrypt all documents and data in the computer system, rendering them unusable by locking them with a code.
5. **The Demand:** The attacker demands a ransom in Bitcoin in exchange for the decryption key to unlock the data.

**Who is targeted:**
The attackers target "Big Game Hunting" targets such as schools, hospitals, and the government. These are the primary targets due to the time-sensitive nature of the operations.
